Working Hours Policy

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear guidelines regarding the working hours at Villa Roma Boutique Hotel and Conference Centre. The policy ensures operational efficiency, maintains a high level of service quality, and provides a structured learning environment for hospitality students from Nkangala TVET College.

Standard Working Hours

Full-Time Staff

  • The standard working week is 40 hours, typically spread across five days.
  • Shifts are divided into morning (6:00 AM - 2:00 PM), afternoon (2:00 PM - 10:00 PM), and night (10:00 PM - 6:00 AM). Employees may be assigned different shifts based on departmental needs.
  • Staff are entitled to a 1-hour unpaid meal break for shifts exceeding 6 hours. Shorter shifts will include a 15-minute paid break.

Part-Time Staff

  • Part-time staff may work up to 30 hours per week, depending on business needs and availability.
  • Shifts may be adjusted based on hotel occupancy, events, and seasonal demand.
  • Part-time staff working more than 4 hours are entitled to a 15-minute paid break.

Student Interns

  • Student shifts will not exceed 32 hours per week to allow sufficient time for academic responsibilities.
  • Training shifts are scheduled between 8:00 AM and 8:00 PM to ensure safety and supervision.
  • Interns will receive practical experience in various departments, including front desk, housekeeping, kitchen, and events, as per their training schedule.

Overtime and Additional Hours

Overtime Policy

  • Overtime will be required during peak periods or special events (e.g., weddings, conferences). It will be compensated at 1.5 times the regular hourly rate.
  • Overtime must be approved by a department manager in advance.

Breaks and Rest Period

Breaks During Shifts

  • Shifts over 6 hours: 1-hour unpaid meal break.
  • Shifts between 4-6 hours: 30-minute paid break.
  • Shifts under 4 hours: No break entitlement.

Rest Periods Between Shifts

  • Employees must have a minimum of 11 hours of rest between shifts.
  • No employee should work more than 6 consecutive days without a rest day.

Shift Scheduling

Shift Assignment and Rotation

  • Shifts are assigned based on business needs, with efforts made to rotate employees equitably across different shifts.
  • Requests for specific shifts or off days should be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance.

Schedule Posting

  • Weekly schedules will be posted every Friday for the following week.
  • Any changes to the schedule will be communicated at least 24 hours in advance.

Shift Swapping

  • Employees may swap shifts with management approval. The employee originally scheduled for the shift remains responsible for ensuring coverage.

Attendance and Punctuality

Expectations

  • Employees and interns are expected to report for duty on time and be fully prepared for their assigned roles.
  • Habitual lateness or absenteeism will be addressed through progressive disciplinary action.

Absence Reporting

  • If unable to attend work due to illness or emergency, employees must notify their manager at least 4 hours before their shift starts.
  • For planned absences, such as leave or appointments, requests should be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance.

Flexible Working Arrangement

Eligibility for Flexible Work

  • Flexible working hours may be granted to employees based on personal circumstances or departmental needs.
  • Requests will be considered case-by-case and must be approved by the department head.

Remote Work

  • Remote work is generally not applicable due to the nature of the hospitality industry but may be allowed for specific administrative roles under special circumstances.

Student Internship Specifics

Training Shifts

  • Training shifts will vary based on department and student progress.
  • Supervisors will provide interns with tasks that align with their educational program, offering practical exposure to hotel operations.

Academic Considerations

  • Interns must balance their training shifts with their academic schedule, ensuring that their work does not interfere with course requirements.
  • Supervisors will support student learning by incorporating feedback into the training modules.

Public Holiday and Special Events

Working on Public Holidays

  • Employees required to work on public holidays will be compensated at twice their normal hourly rate.
  • The hotel will maintain essential operations during public holidays to accommodate guests.

Special Events Coverage

  • During major events (e.g., weddings, conferences), extended shifts may be required, and staff will be informed well in advance.

Policy Review and Updates

This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as needed to reflect changes in labor law, operational requirements, or feedback from staff.

Compliance and Disciplinary Action

Policy Adherence

  • All employees and students are required to adhere to this working hours policy. Violations may lead to disciplinary action, including warnings, suspension, or termination.

Grievances and Appeals

  • Any grievances related to working hours can be reported to the HR department, which will handle them in accordance with the grievance procedure.
